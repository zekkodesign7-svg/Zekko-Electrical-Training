Tools, Cable Crimping, and Lugging. Why do we enforce these rules? Using the right tool for the right job prevents physical accidents, tool failure, and poor craftsmanship. Verifying every single action ensures the quality, stability, and reliable execution of our mechanical and electrical installations before any power is turned on. Remember our core rules: Always choose the exact tool designed for your task. Never use improvised tools, and never use damaged tools with cracked handles or worn tips. After completing any activity, you must physically inspect and verify the quality of the job immediately. Let's look at four key examples on your screen: One. Cable Tray and Panel Mounting. Secure the cable trays and mount the panel firmly on the floor, on a pillar, or on a solid structural base. Panels must always be supported and never left hanging. Confirm the straight position of the device, perform final tightness checks on all bolts, and apply a clear paint marker alignment line across the bolt head and surface. Two. Wire Crimping. Select the exact lug size and matching crimping tool die for your wire gauge. Complete a full crimping cycle, perform a manual pull-test on the lug, and visually inspect to ensure there are no loose copper strands. Three. Control Cable Termination. Insert the ferrule completely into the push-type terminal block. Confirm correct insertion, verify that the wire insulation is not caught inside the metal clamp, and mark a verification line over the wire sleeve. Four. Power Cable Lugging and Termination. Apply heavy-duty power lugs using the correct hydraulic or heavy ratchet tool. Fasten them firmly onto the busbars, confirm the tightness with a calibrated torque wrench, and immediately apply a permanent paint marker line across the nut and busbar interface.These are only the examples.Each and very step you should follow right tool for right job and quality check after termination
