Torque, Tightness, and Paint Marking.  
A secure connection is the foundation of mechanical integrity and electrical safety. Relying on hand-tightening or guesswork is unacceptable in professional installations. Every structural mount, terminal screw, and push-in connection must be executed to precise engineering standards and visually verified. The paint marking you apply is your signature—it is the definitive field proof that the connection has been fully verified and secured.  Let's walk through the explicit technical mandates shown on your screen in image.  One. Equipment and Panel Structural Mounting. Look at the top row of your guide. When executing floor grouting, structural pillar mounting, or fixing heavy equipment and panels, you must always select the correct tools specified for structural fastening. Hand-tightening by guessing is strictly prohibited. Immediately after a mounting bolt or nut successfully achieves its specified torque value, apply a highly visible, continuous paint line directly across the fastener and the mating surface as per standard engineering requirements. Pay close attention to the compulsory training rule: if you are not fully aware of the torque specifications or the correct paint-marking application method for panel structural mountings, you must stop and proactively seek training from your supervisor before executing the task.  Two. Screw-Type Wire Terminations. Moving to the bottom row, when working with screw-type terminal blocks and component connections, use the correctly sized insulated screwdriver or torque screwdriver. This ensures the screw achieves full depth tightness to its exact engineered specification. Once fully tightened, apply a precise, standard paint marking directly onto the screw head or interface. If you require any clarification on the correct marking protocol, stop immediately and request on-the-job training from your supervisor.  Three. Push-In Type Wire Terminations. For toolless, push-in type wire terminations, smoothly and firmly insert the ferrule or wire into the terminal block jaw until it locks. You must immediately perform a physical manual pull-test to ensure the internal spring clamp has gripped the wire perfectly. Once the pull-test confirms a secure connection, look closely at the "Correct Wire Marking Location" diagram at the bottom of in the image. Apply a horizontal paint marking directly onto the wire insulation after the ferrule interface next to the terminal entry point. Do not paint the ferrule itself. If you are unfamiliar with this horizontal wire-marking standard, consult your supervisor for immediate training.  Remember the mandate of the mark: A paint mark is the definitive field proof that a connection is completely secure. If any bolt, screw, or push-in wire lacks its designated marking, it will be treated as completely loose and non-compliant during our quality audits.  Take responsibility for the precision of your work, follow the standards exactly, and ensure every connection is verified and marked correctly.
