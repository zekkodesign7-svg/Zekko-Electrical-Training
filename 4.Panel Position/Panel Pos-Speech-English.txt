Panel and Device Positioning. Let's change our mindset when it comes to positioning. Installation isn't just about putting up a box wherever it fits easily or looks okay to the eye. It is about total precision and safety. First, always look at your drawing. Every panel, junction box, or device has an exact physical coordinate, height, and orientation mapped out for a reason. Verify your site markings against the latest approved layout. Second, ensure a solid foundation. Whether you are mounting on a concrete floor, a heavy structural pillar, or a rigid base frame, the structure must be completely stable. Never let a panel hang loosely or rely on cable trays for support. When you finish fastening, lock that position by drawing a permanent paint marker alignment line across the bolt heads to prove it is fixed for good. Finally, remember this critical rule: if you find a mismatch on-site—if the layout deviates from the drawing, if there is an obstruction, or if the box simply cannot be mounted firmly—do not force it. Do not make unauthorized adjustments on your own. Stop work immediately and inform your supervisor. Let’s do the job right, the first time.
